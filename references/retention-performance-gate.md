# Retention And Performance Gate

Use this for shortform release: Douyin/TikTok/Reels/Shorts/Xiaohongshu/Bilibili, short-drama episodes, promotional ads, and contest videos.

## Rule

Film-grade does not mean slow by default. Shortform needs a strong opening contract, controlled rhythm, and a memorable ending.

## Retention Map

| Time | Job |
|---|---|
| 0-2s | scroll-stop image, situation, question, or contradiction |
| 2-5s | make the viewer understand the game/rule/conflict |
| 5-15s | first escalation or proof |
| 15-30s | reversal, complication, sensory reward, or product reveal |
| 30-60s | emotional turn, payoff, cliffhanger, or CTA |
| final 1-3s | aftertaste image, brand memory, or next-episode hook |

For 3-5 minute films, use this at scene level, not every second. The first scene still needs a promise.

## Platform Bias

| Platform | Bias |
|---|---|
| Douyin / TikTok | fast premise clarity, visual hook, punchy reversal |
| Xiaohongshu | emotion, texture, lifestyle credibility, beauty/detail |
| Bilibili | concept depth, craft, narrative payoff, audience intelligence |
| WeChat / corporate | clarity, trust, brand-safe messaging |
| Contest | brief compliance, memorable concept, production polish |

## Performance QA

- Is the first frame meaningful?
- Can the audience describe the premise after 5 seconds?
- Is there a visible change every 5-10 seconds, or an intentional hold with tension?
- Does sound improve retention even if final subtitles are post-produced?
- Is the ending a memory, not just a stop?
- For ads: is brand/product recall built into the image, not only the caption?

## Downstream Effects

Retention choices affect:

- segment duration
- shot count
- first storyboard frame
- sound cue placement
- final title/CTA/post overlay
- thumbnail or cover frame recommendation
